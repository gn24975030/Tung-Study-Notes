# Mankiw’s Ten Principles of Economics (曼昆的十大經濟學原理)

**Course (課程)**: Principles of Economics 2024
**Topic (主題)**: Mankiw’s Ten Principles (曼昆的十大原理)

Professor Mankiw (the author of our textbook) listed 10 Economic Principles you are expected to learn.
曼昆教授（我們教科書的作者）列出了 10 個你必須學習的經濟學原理。

- **The first 7 are micro principles**: How people make decisions & How people interact with each other.
  **前 7 個是個體經濟學原理**：探討人們如何做決策，以及人們如何互動。
- **The last 3 are macro principles**: Forces that affect the economy as a whole.
  **後 3 個是總體經濟學原理**：探討影響整體經濟的驅動力。

## 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Micro Principles (個體經濟學原理)

### 1. People face trade-off (人們面臨權衡取捨)
Making decisions often requires trading off one goal against another. To get more of one thing, you have to give up something else.
做決策通常需要在不同目標之間取捨。為了得到某樣東西，你必須放棄另一樣東西。
- To have more time studying you have to spend less time on some other activities: leisure, sleep.
  為了有更多時間讀書，你必須減少其他活動的時間：休閒、睡眠。
- To have more economic growth we may have more environmental pollution.
  為了追求更高的經濟成長，我們可能必須忍受更多的環境污染。
- There is a cost: “no such thing as a free lunch.”
  這是有代價的：「天下沒有白吃的午餐」。

### 2. The cost of something is what you give up to get it (某樣東西的成本是你為了得到它所放棄的東西)
Decision-makers have to consider both the **explicit** and **implicit** costs of their actions.
決策者必須同時考慮其行為的**外顯成本**與**隱含成本**。
- **Explicit costs (外顯成本)**: out-of-pocket expenses (實際支付的金錢費用)。
- **Implicit costs (隱含成本)**: costs with no financial outlay, e.g. time costs (沒有實際金錢支出的成本，例如時間成本)。
- *Question (思考)*: If someone gives you a free ticket to a concert, does it mean your cost of attending the concert is zero?
  如果有人給你一張免費的演唱會門票，你去聽演唱會的成本真的是零嗎？(答案：不是，因為你付出了時間)。

### 3. Rational people think at the margin (理性的人進行邊際思考)
This basically means rational people weight the [marginal] cost and the [marginal] benefit of an action when they make decisions.
這基本上意味著理性的人在做決策時，會權衡一項行動的邊際成本與邊際效益。

### 4. People respond to incentives (人們會對誘因做出反應)
Behavior changes when costs or benefits change.
當成本或效益改變時，行為也會跟著改變。
- *Example (例子)*: “Everyone will get an A in this class automatically. There will be no exams and you don’t even have to show up for class.” How would you choose to spend your time for the rest of the term?
  「這堂課每個人都會自動拿 A。沒有考試，你甚至不用來上課。」在學期剩下的時間裡，你會如何選擇分配你的時間？

### 5. Trade can make everyone better off (貿易能使每個人都變得更好)
- Trade allows countries to **specialize** in activities that they do best and become more efficient.
  貿易允許國家**專業化**於他們最擅長的活動，從而變得更有效率。
- Trade between two countries (firms, families, or individuals) can make both better off.
  兩個國家（或企業、家庭、個人）之間的貿易可以創造雙贏。
- By trading with others, people can buy a greater variety of goods or services.
  透過與他人貿易，人們可以買到更多樣化的商品或服務。

### 6. Markets are usually a good way to organize economic activity (市場通常是組織經濟活動的好方法)
- Market mechanism (vs. central planning) is usually the best way to allocate resources efficiently.
  市場機制（相對於中央計畫）通常是有效分配資源的最佳方式。
- Market participants (households and firms) acting on their self-interest tend to generate and use information more efficiently, as if they are guided by an "**invisible hand**".
  市場參與者（家庭與企業）基於自身利益行動，往往能更有效地產生和使用資訊，彷彿被一隻**「看不見的手」**所引導。

### 7. Government can sometimes improve market outcomes (政府有時能改善市場結果)
- Markets operate better when the gov’t enforces rules and maintain order: enforcement of property rights, regulations against monopolies and pollution, and promote equality, etc.
  當政府執行規則並維持秩序時（例如：執行財產權、管制壟斷與污染、促進平等等），市場運作得更好。
- In some situations the market mechanism may fail to allocate resources efficiently – **market failure**.
  在某些情況下，市場機制可能無法有效分配資源，這稱為**市場失靈**。
- The government can change the outcome through public policies.
  政府可以透過公共政策來改變結果。

## 🌍 Macro Principles (總體經濟學原理)

### 8. A country’s standard of living depends on its ability to produce goods and services (一國的生活水準取決於其生產商品與服務的能力)
- Countries whose workers produce a large quantity of goods and services per unit of time enjoy a high standard of living.
  如果一個國家的勞工能在單位時間內生產大量的商品與服務，該國就能享有較高的生活水準。
- Similarly, as a nation's **productivity** grows, so does its average income.
  同理，隨著一個國家**生產力**的成長，其平均收入也會隨之增加。

### 9. Prices rise when the government prints too much money (當政府印製太多貨幣時，物價會上漲)
When a government creates large quantities of the nation's money, the value of the money falls and prices will increase as a result.
當政府創造了大量的國家貨幣時，貨幣的價值就會下降，結果導致物價上漲（通貨膨脹）。

### 10. Society faces a short-run trade-off between inflation and unemployment (社會面臨通貨膨脹與失業之間的短期權衡)
Reducing inflation often causes a temporary rise in unemployment. This tradeoff is crucial for understanding the short-run effects of changes in taxes, government spending and monetary policy.
降低通貨膨脹通常會導致失業率短暫上升。這種權衡關係對於理解稅收、政府支出和貨幣政策變化的短期影響至關重要。
